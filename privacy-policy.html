Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. We may share your information with trusted third-party service providers in the limited circumstances described below.

5.1 Supabase (Backend Infrastructure)

Our Site uses Supabase (Supabase Inc., headquartered in San Francisco, California, USA) as our backend database and authentication provider. All form submissions from our contact form and client intake form are stored in Supabase-hosted databases. Supabase acts as a data processor on our behalf and is bound by its own Privacy Policy and data processing agreements.

Supabase stores data in data centers located in the United States. For users in the EEA or UK, this constitutes an international data transfer — please see Section 11.

5.2 Google Fonts

Our Site loads fonts via Google Fonts (Google LLC). When your browser requests these fonts, Google's servers receive your IP address and browser metadata. This is a standard, industry-wide practice. Please refer to Google's Privacy Policy for details on how Google handles this data.

5.3 Payment Processors

If you engage our services, payment processing is handled by third-party providers (such as Stripe, PayPal, or bank transfer). We do not store payment card details on our systems. Those processors operate under their own privacy policies and PCI-DSS compliance frameworks.

5.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your personal information if required to do so by law or in good faith belief that such action is necessary to: (a) comply with a legal obligation or valid legal process; (b) protect and defend our rights or property; (c) prevent or investigate possible wrongdoing in connection with our services; or (d) protect the personal safety of users or the public.

5.5 Business Transfers

In the event BOOKISTUDIO is acquired, merged with, or its assets are transferred to another entity, your personal information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email or prominent notice on our Site prior to your information becoming subject to a different privacy policy.

5.6 No Other Sharing

Beyond the situations listed above, we do not share your personal information with any other third party without your prior explicit consent.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law.

  • Contact form inquiries: Retained for up to 3 years from the date of submission, unless you request earlier deletion or we engage in an ongoing business relationship.
  • Client intake forms: Retained for the duration of the client relationship, plus up to 5 years thereafter, to comply with record-keeping obligations and to handle any disputes.
  • Active client project data: Retained for the duration of the project plus 5 years, for business and legal compliance purposes.
  • Server and access logs: Automatically purged within 90 days by Supabase infrastructure.

When your data is no longer needed, we will securely delete or anonymize it. You may request early deletion at any time — see Section 9 for your rights.

Security

We take the security of your personal information seriously and implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect it against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

7.1 Technical Measures

  • HTTPS encryption: All data transmitted between your browser and our Site is encrypted via TLS/SSL.
  • Database security: Our Supabase database uses Row Level Security (RLS) policies, ensuring that public users can only submit data — not read or modify existing records without authentication.
  • Access controls: Administrative access to the database requires authenticated credentials and is restricted to authorized personnel only.
  • Minimal data exposure: The anon key embedded in our client-side code is intentionally limited in scope; RLS enforces strict access rules at the database level.

7.2 Limitations

No method of data transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by applicable law.

7.3 Breach Notification

If a security breach occurs involving your personal data, we will notify affected individuals within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (where feasible), and will notify the appropriate supervisory authority where required by law (e.g., under GDPR Article 33).

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

8.1 Session Storage & Local Storage

Our Site may use browser session storage or local storage to maintain your session state and preferences within the scope of a single browsing session. This data is not transmitted to external servers and is not used for tracking across sessions.

8.2 Third-Party Cookies

Third-party services we integrate (such as Google Fonts) may set cookies or use similar tracking technologies in your browser. We do not control these third-party cookies. You can disable or manage cookies through your browser settings at any time without loss of core site functionality.

8.3 Analytics

We currently do not deploy any analytics platforms (such as Google Analytics) on our Site. If we add analytics in the future, this policy will be updated accordingly, and we will implement appropriate consent mechanisms where required by law.

8.4 Do Not Track

Our Site does not currently respond to browser "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals, as there is no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT. We do not engage in cross-site tracking.

8.5 Managing Cookies

You can control and/or delete cookies through your browser settings. Removing or blocking cookies may affect the functionality of some features on our Site. For instructions on how to manage cookies, please visit your browser's help documentation or allaboutcookies.org.

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights with respect to your personal information. We honor these rights regardless of where you are located.

9.1 Rights Available to All Users

  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to Deletion ("Right to Be Forgotten"): You may request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain legal exceptions (e.g., information we must retain for legal or contractual obligations).
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request that we limit how we use your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: Where applicable, you may request that we provide your personal information in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: You may object to the processing of your personal information where we rely on legitimate interests as the legal basis.

9.2 Additional Rights for California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • Right to Know: The right to know what personal information is collected, used, shared, or sold.
  • Right to Delete: The right to request deletion of your personal information.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale: The right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. We do not sell personal information.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: You have the right not to receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your CCPA rights.
  • Right to Correct: The right to correct inaccurate personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information: The right to limit the use and disclosure of sensitive personal information.

To exercise any California privacy right, please contact us using the details in Section 13. We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days.

9.3 Additional Rights for EEA/UK Residents (GDPR/UK GDPR)

If you are located in the EEA or the UK, you have additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or UK GDPR, including the rights listed in Section 9.1. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority if you believe we have not handled your personal data in accordance with applicable law.

  • EU residents may contact their national data protection authority.
  • UK residents may contact the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

9.4 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of your rights, please email us at privacy@bookistudio.com with the subject line "Privacy Rights Request." We will respond within 30 days and may need to verify your identity before processing your request. We will not charge a fee for handling reasonable requests.
